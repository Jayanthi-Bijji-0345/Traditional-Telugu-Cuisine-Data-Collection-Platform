# User Acquisition Strategy  
**Submitted by Team10-1**

## Audience Segments

We aim to engage contributors from varied backgrounds who are familiar with or curious about Telugu culinary traditions, including:

- Elder home cooks with oral recipes passed down generations.  
- Local vendors, street-side chefs, and mess cooks.  
- Young volunteers and college students documenting food culture.  
- Telugu diaspora keen on preserving their food heritage.  
- NGOs and cultural collectives working in rural areas.  

## Data Contribution Routes

Our platform will be open to multiple types of inputs from users and interns alike:

- **Typed Recipes** – Users can submit step-by-step recipes in Telugu/English.  
- **Photo Submissions** – Upload images of ingredients, cooking stages, or final dishes.  
- **Voice Notes** – Recordings of people explaining or narrating recipes.  
- **Video Clips** – Home-style or market-style food preparation.  
- **Mixed Entries** – Allowing a combination of the above for rich documentation.

Role-based logins will ensure separate flows for interns and public users.

## Community Outreach Plan

To get quality data from diverse sources, our outreach strategy includes:

- **Local Networks** – Connect through student clubs, language departments, and food interest groups.  
- **Field Visits** – Visit households, canteens, and markets in person for interviews.  
- **Phone-Based Assistance** – Help elderly users submit content via guided calls.  
- **Digital Campaigns** – Promote through WhatsApp circles, campus fests, and community forums.  
- **Referral Incentives** – Reward consistent contributors with recognition or shout-outs.

## Retention and Motivation Tools

To encourage ongoing contributions and deepen involvement:

- **Contributor Highlights** – Feature top or consistent contributors on the home page.  
- **Milestone Badges** – Automatically award titles like “5 Recipes Logged” or “Audio Archivist.”  
- **Weekly Challenges** – Lighthearted topics like “Monsoon Specials” or “Festival Foods.”  
- **Engagement Features** – Commenting, reactions, and sharing within the app.

## Review & Feedback

We will actively monitor and improve user experience using:

- **Mini-Reviews** – Weekly internal content checks for quality and diversity.  
- **User Voice** – Anonymous suggestion boxes and casual check-ins via phone or form.  
- **Test Runs** – Regular feedback from fellow volunteers testing app features.  
- **Expert Input** – Where possible, validate selected content with food historians or chefs.

## Timeline (4 Weeks)

| Week | Focus                               |
|------|-------------------------------------|
| 1    | Prepare data forms, portal, and outreach material  
| 2    | Onboard initial users and begin campus/field visits  
| 3    | Launch public collection and scale online engagement

## Consent and Data Handling

- Clear consent process before using any photo, audio, or video.  
- Personal data anonymized if requested.  
- All files stored securely with backups and metadata.  
- Data solely used for language model training and cultural preservation.
